Mario Kart 8 Tournament Rules

General rules

  • You can choose your character, vehicule and 1 race course per stage.
    You can think about what course you want before, if you dont pick, it will be random.
  • 4 players will race 4 courses (Grandprix).
  • The 2 highest ranked players move on to the next round.
  • In the final, the participants will play 8 races instead of 4.
  • Only Hyper Island studennts or alumni allowed.

1. Tournament format

  • Free for all
  • 4 people per switch
  • 4 races per round
  • The 2 highest ranked players move on to the next round
  • In the final, the participants will play 8 races instead of 4

1.2 Validation of results

  • At the end of a round, participants must immediately report the result of the round to the “judge”.

1.3 Rules related to the proceedings

  • Ties will be broken by a single race between participants (same rules).
  • If the number of participants is not a multiple of 4, some games will be randomly reduced from 4 to 3 players so that all the participants can play.
  • Participants have 10 minutes to arrive for their game when they are called or they automatically lose the game in progress.
  • A participant may not be replaced for a game or during a game.

2. Map Picking

  • Before each race, each player chooses the map they like to play for that race.
  • A map cannot be chosen more than once during the same round.

3. Race Configurations

  • Type of game: Multiplayer
  • Game mode: VS Race
  • Speed: 150cc Race
  • Teams: No Teams
  • Items: Normal Items
  • COM: Hard COM or No COM?
  • COM Vehicles: All Vehicles
  • Courses: Choose
  • Race Count: 4 Races

4. Interruptions

  • It is prohibited from leaving an ongoing game unless it is permitted by the format of the game or expressly authorized by an administrator.
  • If a participant breaks the previous rule by leaving the game before its end, the person will be considered to have finished in 4th place in the set.
  • Pressing pause (main menu button) is only allowed if all participants agree to do so.
  • Even if the above condition is met, it is permitted to pause only in one of the following cases:
    • One of the participants must absolutely speak to an administrator.
    • A problem occurs with the equipment (e.g., crashing, controller disconnection).
    • An emergency beyond the control of the participants occurs (e.g., fire alarm).
  • If the two previous articles are not respected, the participant who pressed pause will be considered to have finished in 4th place in the set.
  • If one of the participants considers they have been the victim of a violation of the rules during a game, the participants must not stop playing before the game is over. Once the game is over, a participant can request an investigation from the administrators according to section 8.
  • In any case of interruption, administrators can make the decision to restart a game if they wish to do so.
